Milestones

Key moments in SUIT’s growth and recognition.

2007

SUIT is Founded

The Service User Involvement Team is established in Wolverhampton in February 2007 as a project of WVCA (Wolverhampton Voluntary & Community Action), giving people with lived experience of addiction a voice and platform for peer-led support.

2014

Queen's Award for Voluntary Services

SUIT receives the prestigious Queen's Award for Voluntary Services, signed by Her Majesty the Queen, for "training recovering addicts and empowering those that are suffering from addiction to move forward with their lives, contributing positively to themselves and society as a whole."

2018

European Recognition — BeTrAd

SUIT is recognised by the BeTrAd (EU) as a model of good practice for lived experience recovery support across Europe.

2019

European Model of Good Practice — Street Support Project

The Street Support Project (EU) recognises SUIT as a European Model of Good Practice in January 2019, placing Wolverhampton's recovery community on the international stage.

2018

Partnership with Recovery Near You

SUIT begins formally working alongside Recovery Near You, Wolverhampton's clinical treatment provider, combining lived experience with clinical treatment for optimum recovery outcomes.

2020

Cultural Engagement Programme Launches

SUIT launches its cultural engagement programme, with Punjabi-speaking and Polish-speaking project workers joining the team to break down language barriers and reach diverse communities across Wolverhampton.

2021

Creative Arts & Research Programme

SUIT establishes its Creative Arts & Research Lead role, embedding arts, drama, spoken word, and community art projects as core recovery pathways. Collaborations with Geese Theatre, Asylum Artist Quarter, and the Wolverhampton Literature Festival begin.

2022

Hospital DALT Ward Peer Support

SUIT establishes peer support presence on the Drug & Alcohol Liaison Team (DALT) ward at New Cross Hospital, bringing lived experience directly to patients with severe complications from alcohol and drugs.

2023

DDN National Conference Speakers

SUIT staff present at the 15th DDN Conference at the National Motorcycle Museum, Birmingham. Marcus Johnson, Karolina Sowinska, and Christiane Jenkins share SUIT's innovative approach to national audiences. Featured in DDN Magazine.

2023

CLERO Membership

SUIT becomes a proud member of the College of Lived Experience Recovery Organisations (CLERO), connecting with other national LEROs and committing to government standards for peer-led support.

2024

Recovery Ambassador Programme

SUIT launches its structured Recovery Ambassador Programme, a formal volunteering pathway that trains people in recovery as peer mentors with qualifications, regular supervisions, and career development support.

2024

Wolverhampton PRIDE Partnership

SUIT attends Wolverhampton PRIDE alongside Recovery Near You, raising awareness of addiction support with colourful and creative outreach stalls celebrating inclusivity, identity, and freedom.

2025

Favor UK National Recovery Conference

SUIT delivers a memorable presentation at the Favor UK National Recovery Conference at Molineux Stadium, featuring immersive theatre, lived experience shares, and artwork by SUIT, Recovery Near You, and Good Shepherd clients.

2025

National Recovery Walk — Wolverhampton

Wolverhampton hosts the National Recovery Walk at West Park. The Favor UK event celebrates the unity, diversity, creativity, and strength of the recovery community, with SUIT at the heart of the organisation.

2025

LEAG — Lived Experience Advisory Group

SUIT establishes the Lived Experience Advisory Group (LEAG) — quarterly meetings ensuring service users' views are not just heard, but acted upon. The December LEAG at Molineux Stadium celebrates volunteer achievements in the Recovery Ambassador programme.

2026

Published in the British Library

SUIT staff attend the British Library in London for the launch of "Lived Experience Recovery Organisations" co-authored by Dame Carol Black and Dr Ed Day. SUIT's chapter contribution is deposited in the National Archives, ensuring their work becomes part of Britain's permanent record.
