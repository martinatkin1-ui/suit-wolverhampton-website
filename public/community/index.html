Mental Health Peer Support Drop-Ins — Monthly at the Station Hub

A safe space open to all who may need support, advice, and community links to services including health & wellbeing, advocacy, housing, welfare, education & training, employment, activities & groups, addiction & recovery, mental health, and delivering peer-led initiatives. Workshops are themed with activities and delivered by staff and volunteers working with SUIT, the Good Shepherd, One Wolverhampton, Wolverhampton City Council's Department of Public Health, Rethink, and the NHS. First Friday of every month, 10am-12pm at Train Station Hub WV1 1LE.

Jan 24
Art Pinned #exhibition #animation #asylum-artist-quarter

[P R O] F I L E Residency — 'Joey the Fish' at Wolverhampton Art Gallery

SUIT/Recovery Near You client David's animation work 'Joey the Fish' went live at Wolverhampton Art Gallery on 24th January 2026. The installation is a deep dive into David's lived experience of struggles with alcohol and its consequences, and how he swam to the top again. The project, delivered by Asylum Artist Quarter and funded by Arts Council England, gives SUIT clients an incredible experience — undertaking a programme where they can access artist studios and skills workshops.

"Joey inhabits an ocean disrupted by urbanisation and human neglect. A discarded beer can frequently appears — a small, ordinary object that carries disproportionate weight. It signals addiction, harm, and the quiet normalisation of behaviour that can have serious personal and environmental consequences." — Wolverhampton Art Gallery

Events Pinned #milestone #national #publication

SUIT Book Chapter Placed in the British Library National Archives

In January 2026, staff from SUIT attended the British Library in London to help launch a groundbreaking new book on addiction and recovery. The book, 'Lived Experience Recovery Organisations', was co-authored by internationally renowned experts Dame Carol Black and Dr Ed Day. Created by Dr David Patton and Dot Smith of Recovery Connections, the book has been deposited in the British Library, ensuring that SUIT's work is recognised as a meaningful contribution to national knowledge of lived experience recovery.

"To have our work recognised in such a prestigious publication really does validate, not only what we do here in Wolverhampton, but what the LERO movement is achieving nationally." — Marcus Johnson, SUIT Project Manager

Community Liver Scan Clinics with Recovery Near You & Cultural Engagement Team

Over 1,200 liver scans have been completed at a pioneering series of pop-up clinics across Wolverhampton designed to bring non-invasive health screening directly into the community. By teaming up with Recovery Near You and SUIT, and working alongside faith leaders, lived experience volunteers, businesses, and community organisations, the programme has demonstrated the value of culturally sensitive, accessible, and health-focused interventions in breaking down stigma.

"These community Fibro-scan clinics are a fantastic example of how we are working with partners to bring healthcare into familiar, welcoming spaces." — Councillor Obaida Ahmed, Cabinet Member for Health

Mar 15
Announcements #leag #governance #service-user-voice

LEAG — Lived Experience Advisory Group Launches Quarterly Meetings

SUIT launched the Lived Experience Advisory Group (LEAG), a formal structure ensuring that service users' voices directly shape the services they receive. Quarterly meetings bring together service users, staff, and partner organisations to review feedback, set priorities, and hold services accountable. The 'You said, we did' format ensures transparency and genuine co-production.

Recovery Ambassador Programme — From Service User to Mentor

SUIT's structured Recovery Ambassador Programme continues to transform lives by helping people in recovery build skills, gain qualifications, and find purpose through peer-led volunteering. Ambassadors are supported with relapse prevention plans, regular supervision, and relevant training. Opportunities include facilitating groups, providing one-to-one support, and outreach at New Cross Hospital, Recovery Near You, and community events.

Announcements #hospital #dalt #nhs

Hospital DALT Service — Peer Support on the Wards

SUIT launched its Hospital DALT (Drug & Alcohol Liaison Team) service at New Cross Hospital, with Matt Whittall providing peer support directly on hospital wards. This groundbreaking initiative puts lived experience support at the heart of clinical treatment, catching people at their most vulnerable and connecting them to recovery services before discharge.

SMART Recovery Groups — Mutual Aid at SUIT

SUIT's weekly SMART Recovery groups provide a science-based, self-empowering approach to addiction recovery. Facilitated by Warren Sutherland, sessions focus on building motivation, coping with urges, managing thoughts and feelings, and living a balanced life. Open to anyone in the Wolverhampton community.
