How We Help

No jargon. No waiting lists. Just real people providing real support. Here's what we do.

Peer Mentoring & Buddy Support

Regular check-in calls, guidance, care plans, and someone to listen. Our Buddy Support service pairs you with a peer mentor who truly understands.

Advocacy & Wraparound Support

Help with housing and homelessness, benefits (PIP/UC/ESA/JSA), form filling, debt advice, job searches, CV writing, and signposting to partner organisations.

Drop-In Sanctuary

A safe, warm place to be. Monday & Friday 10:00-15:00. Free tea, coffee, and real support from people who've been there. No appointment needed.

Creative Arts & Recovery

Art therapy, drama projects with Geese Theatre, spoken word, music sessions, Wolverhampton Literature Festival participation, and community exhibitions.

Clinical Treatment Pathway

We work closely with Recovery Near You to support your clinical treatment. We help remove barriers to healthcare, attend appointments with you, and support prescriptions.

Volunteering & Recovery Ambassadors

Our structured Recovery Ambassador Programme helps people in recovery build skills, gain qualifications, and find purpose through peer-led volunteering.

Peer Mentoring & Buddy Support

Talk to someone who has actually beaten addiction. Every peer mentor at SUIT has lived experience of recovery. Regular check-in calls, guidance, care plans, and someone to listen.

Our Recovery Ambassadors provide empathy and genuine connection. They offer practical, first-hand insights and inspire hope by demonstrating that recovery is possible.

"Until you become inspired by someone else, the change can't happen. We have to see it through our own eyes." — Jason Spreckley, Outreach Lead

Advocacy & Wraparound Support

Navigating housing, benefits, and services is overwhelming — especially when you're in recovery. Our advocacy team will champion your rights and fight your corner.

  • Housing support and homeless issues
  • Benefits support — PIP, UC, ESA, JSA applications & appeals
  • Form filling including UC50, Housing, Job applications
  • Debt advice and setting up payment plans
  • Job searches and CV writing support
  • Referrals to Changing Lives, Good Shepherd, Wolves Foundation, Rethink
  • Support for prison leavers
  • Outreach support & home visits
